# Neighborhood: Lower Haight / Hayes Valley border

287-291 Page Street sits on the 200 block of Page Street, right on the edge
between Hayes Valley and Lower Haight. Redfin tags the location under
Northwest San Francisco and Lower Haight. Page Street here is a designated
Slow Street, meaning through-traffic is calmed and pedestrians and cyclists
have priority.

The building looks across Page at the Laguna mini-park, a public park that
gives the facade a rare open view for a city block. The house is also
detached on three sides, which is unusual in this part of San Francisco.

## Historical context

In 1885, when this house was built, Hayes Valley was the western edge of
settled San Francisco. Beyond it, sand dunes ran out to Ocean Beach. The
block was a popular spot for new Victorian mansions built in the Eastlake
Stick style. Many of the architectural details of the era were ordered from
millwork catalogs, including the kind of turned spindles, incised panels,
ceiling medallions, and patterned hinges that appear on this house.

141 years later, the neighborhood is central rather than fringe. It sits in
the geographic and cultural middle of the city.

## Feel of the block

Detached on three sides with garden outlook, radiant heat, double parlors,
and a rear garden with outdoor kitchen. The location combines quiet Slow
Street traffic with five-minute access to the Symphony, the Opera, and the
101.
